Our Story

Founded on Vision,
Built on Excellence

Kalinga Institute of Industrial Technology (KIIT) is a private deemed university located in Bhubaneswar, Odisha, India. Founded in 1992 by Prof. Achyuta Samanta with just 12 students and a ₹5,000 seed fund, KIIT has grown into one of India's most prestigious private universities.

In 2004, KIIT gained deemed university status under Section 3 of the UGC Act 1956 — becoming the youngest institution in India to receive this recognition. Guided by the motto "Sā Vidyā Yā Vimuktayē" (Knowledge is that which liberates), KIIT promotes innovation, research, and holistic development.

Today, KIIT spans 25 sq. km across 23 campuses, offers 200+ programs through 28 constituent schools, and attracts students from over 65 countries. It has received NAAC A++ accreditation — the highest grade in India.

Campus & Infrastructure

A City of Learning

KIIT's sprawling campus covers 25 sq. km across 23 faculty-specific campuses in Bhubaneswar — the temple city of Odisha. With 12 million sq. ft. of built-up area, it is one of India's largest university campuses.

The campus is solar-powered, fully Wi-Fi enabled, and features world-class labs, libraries, sports facilities, medical centres, hostels, and a dedicated Technology Business Incubator (KIIT-TBI) backed by DST.
